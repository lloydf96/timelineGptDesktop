April 5, 1993: Nvidia Corporation is founded by Jensen Huang, Chris Malachowsky, and Curtis Priem.
- January 22, 1999: Nvidia goes public.
- 1998: Nvidia releases the RIVA TNT graphics adapter.
- Late 1999: Nvidia releases the GeForce 256, introducing on-board transformation and lighting to consumer-level 3D hardware.
- Summer 2000: Nvidia releases the GeForce2 GTS.
- December 2000: Nvidia acquires the intellectual assets of its one-time rival 3dfx.
- July 2002: Nvidia acquires Exluna.
- August 2003: Nvidia acquires MediaQ.
- April 22, 2004: Nvidia acquires iReady.
- December 2004: Nvidia announces it will assist Sony with the design of the graphics processor in the PlayStation 3 game console.
- December 14, 2005: Nvidia acquires ULI Electronics.
- March 2006: Nvidia acquires Hybrid Graphics.
- December 2006: Nvidia and AMD receive subpoenas from the U.S. Department of Justice regarding possible antitrust violations in the graphics card industry.
- January 5, 2007: Nvidia completes the acquisition of PortalPlayer, Inc.
- February 2008: Nvidia acquires Ageia, developer of the PhysX physics engine and physics processing unit.
- July 2008: Nvidia takes a write-down of approximately $200 million on its first-quarter revenue due to manufacturing defects in certain mobile chipsets and GPUs.
- September 2008: Nvidia becomes subject of a class action lawsuit over defects in certain laptop models
- September 2010: Nvidia reaches a settlement in the class action lawsuit
- January 10, 2011: Nvidia signs a six-year, $1.5 billion cross-licensing agreement with Intel
- November 2011: Nvidia releases Tegra 3 ARM system-on-a-chip for mobile devices
- May 2011: Nvidia acquires Icera, a baseband chip making company in the UK, for $367 million
- January 2013: Nvidia unveils Tegra 4 and Nvidia Shield, an Android-based handheld game console
- July 29, 2013: Nvidia acquires PGI from STMicroelectronics
- May 6, 2016: Nvidia unveils the first GPUs of the GeForce 10 series, the GTX 1080 and 1070
- July 2016: Nvidia agrees to a settlement for a false advertising lawsuit regarding its GTX 970 model
- May 2017: Nvidia announces a partnership with Toyota for autonomous vehicles
- July 2017: Nvidia and Baidu announce a partnership for AI in various industries
- December 7, 2017: Nvidia releases the Titan V
- March 27, 2018: Nvidia releases the Nvidia Quadro GV100
- September 27, 2018: Nvidia releases the RTX 2080 GPUs
- May 2018: Nvidia users request web drivers for legacy Mac Pro machines running macOS Mojave
- March 11, 2019: Nvidia announces a deal to buy Mellanox Technologies for $6.9 billion
- May 2019: Nvidia announces new RTX Studio laptops
- August 2019: Nvidia announces Minecraft RTX
- May 2020: Nvidia's top scientists develop an open-source ventilator for COVID-19
- May 14, 2020: Nvidia announces Ampere GPU microarchitecture and Nvidia A100 GPU accelerator
- July 2020: Reports surface that Nvidia is in talks to buy Arm from SoftBank for $32 billion
- September 1, 2020: Nvidia officially announces the GeForce 30 series
- September 13, 2020: Nvidia announces plans to buy Arm from SoftBank for $40 billion
- October 2020: Nvidia announces plans to build the most powerful computer in Cambridge, England
- October 2020: Nvidia announces the retirement of its workstation GPU brand Quadro and the shift to Nvidia RTX for future products.
- October 2020: Nvidia announces the development of a supercomputer named Cambridge-1 to support healthcare research.
- August 2021: The proposed takeover of Arm Holdings by Nvidia is stalled due to competition concerns raised by the UK's Competition and Markets Authority.
- October 2021: The European Commission opens a competition investigation into Nvidia's acquisition of Arm Holdings.
- February 2022: SoftBank and Nvidia announce that they will not move forward with the Arm Holdings acquisition due to significant regulatory challenges.
- March 2022: Nvidia's CEO mentions that they are open to having Intel manufacture their chips in the future.
- April 2022: Nvidia plans to open a new research center in Yerevan, Armenia.
- September 2022: Nvidia announces its next-generation automotive-grade chip, Drive Thor.
- October 2022: The U.S. Department of Commerce places an embargo on exports to China of advanced microchips, including Nvidia's data center chip.
- November 2022: Nvidia unveils a new advanced chip in China, called the A800 GPU, that meets the export control rules.
- 2009: Nvidia GPUs are used in the "big bang" of deep learning, leading to a 100x increase in speed for deep-learning systems.
- April 2016: Nvidia releases the DGX-1, a supercomputer based on an 8 GPU cluster, to improve deep learning capabilities.
- November 2016: Google Cloud installs Nvidia Tesla K80 and P100 GPU-based virtual machines.
- Late 2016: Microsoft adds GPU servers in a preview offering of its N series based on Nvidia's Tesla K80s.
- Late 2016: AWS's P2 instance is produced using up to 16 Nvidia Tesla K80 GPUs.
- 2017: Nvidia's deep learning technology is used at the Riken Center for Advanced Intelligence Project for Fujitsu, leading to a boost in earnings.
- May 2018: Nvidia researchers create a system that allows a robot to learn a job by observing a person doing the same job.
- March 2018: Nvidia's Inception Program has 2,800 startups.
- August 2021: Nvidia Inception has over 8,500 members in 90 countries, with cumulative funding of $60 billion (USD).
- January 2015: Issues with the GeForce GTX 970's specifications are brought up by users.
- February 2015: Nvidia issues a statement that the card's initially announced specifications had been altered without notice before the card was made commercially available.
- February 2015: A class-action lawsuit alleging false advertising is filed against Nvidia and Gigabyte Technology in the U.S. District Court for Northern California.
- July 2016: Nvidia agrees to a preliminary settlement of the U.S. class action lawsuit, offering a $30 refund on GTX 970 purchases.
- 2015: Maxwell-based cards do not perform well when async compute is utilized, leading to a partial implementation of the feature through a driver-based shim.
- March 1, 2018: Nvidia announces the GeForce Partner Program.
- May 4, 2018: Nvidia cancels the GeForce Partner Program.
- December 10, 2020: Nvidia tells Hardware Unboxed that it will no longer supply them with GeForce Founders Edition graphics card review units.
- December 12, 2020: Prominent technology reviewers criticize Nvidia's move to dictate specific outcomes of technology reviews.
- December 14, 2020: Nvidia apologizes to Hardware Unboxed and walks back its decision to withhold review units.
- May 2022: Nvidia agrees to pay $5.5 million to settle civil charges by the SEC for failing to disclose the impact of cryptomining on its business.
